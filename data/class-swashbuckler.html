Swashbuckler

ACG Hybrid Class (Fighter/Gunslinger)

Role

Swashbucklers are nimble, charming duelists who fight with panache and finesse. They use a pool of panache points — replenished by critical hits and kills — to fuel daring deeds that give them mobility and precision. They excel with light blades and no armor, adding their Charisma to AC and dealing devastating precise strikes.

Alignment

Any.

Hit Died10
BABFull (1 per level)
Good SavesFortitude, Reflex
Poor SavesWill
Skill Ranks4 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Acrobatics (Dex), Bluff (Cha), Climb (Str), Craft (Int), Diplomacy (Cha), Escape Artist (Dex), Intimidate (Cha), Knowledge (local) (Int), Knowledge (nobility) (Int), Perception (Wis), Perform (Cha), Profession (Wis), Ride (Dex), Sense Motive (Wis), Sleight of Hand (Dex), Swim (Str).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Swashbucklers are proficient with all simple and martial weapons, light armor, and bucklers. They are not proficient with medium armor, heavy armor, or non-buckler shields.

Class Features

Panache (Ex)

More than just a lightly armored warrior, a swashbuckler is a daring combatant. She fights with panache: a fluctuating measure of a swashbuckler's ability to perform amazing actions in combat. At the start of each day, a swashbuckler gains a number of panache points equal to her Charisma modifier (minimum 1). Her panache goes up or down throughout the day, but usually cannot go higher than her Charisma modifier (minimum 1), though feats and magic can affect this. A swashbuckler spends and gains panache through deeds and the following methods.

Critical Hit with a Light or One-Handed Piercing Melee Weapon: Each time the swashbuckler confirms a critical hit with a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon, she regains 1 panache point. Confirming a critical hit on a helpless or unaware creature or a creature with fewer HD than half the swashbuckler's level doesn't restore panache.

Killing Blow with a Light or One-Handed Piercing Melee Weapon: When the swashbuckler reduces a creature to 0 or fewer hit points with a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon attack while in the heat of combat, she regains 1 panache point. Destroying an unattended object, reducing a helpless or unaware creature to 0 hp, or reducing a creature with fewer HD than half the swashbuckler's level doesn't restore panache.

Deeds (Ex)

Swashbucklers spend panache points to accomplish deeds. Most deeds grant the swashbuckler a momentary bonus or effect, but some provide longer-lasting effects.

Derring-Do (Ex), 1st level: The swashbuckler can spend 1 panache point when she makes an Acrobatics, Climb, Escape Artist, Fly, Ride, or Swim check to roll 1d6 and add the result to the check. She can do this after she makes the check but before the result is revealed. If the result of the d6 roll is a natural 6, she rolls another 1d6 and adds it to the check. She can continue to do this as long as she rolls natural 6s, up to a total number of times equal to her Dexterity modifier (minimum 1).

Dodging Panache (Ex), 1st level: When an opponent attempts a melee attack against the swashbuckler, she can as an immediate action spend 1 panache point to move 5 feet; if this movement takes her out of the attacker's reach, the attack automatically misses. This movement doesn't count as a 5-foot step.

Opportune Parry and Riposte (Ex), 1st level: When an opponent makes a melee attack against the swashbuckler, she can spend 1 panache point and expend a use of an attack of opportunity to attempt to parry that attack. She makes an attack roll as if she were making an attack of opportunity; for each size category the attacking creature is larger than the swashbuckler, the swashbuckler takes a –2 penalty on this roll. If her roll equals or exceeds the attacker's roll, the attack is parried. If she parried the attack and has at least 1 panache point, she can make an attack against the creature that attacked her as an immediate action (this riposte does not consume another attack of opportunity).

Kip-Up (Ex), 3rd level: While the swashbuckler has at least 1 panache point, she can kip-up from prone as a move action without provoking an attack of opportunity. She can kip-up as a swift action by spending 1 panache point.

Menacing Swordplay (Ex), 3rd level: While she has at least 1 panache point, when the swashbuckler hits an opponent with a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon, she can choose to use Intimidate to demoralize that opponent as a swift action instead of a standard action.

Precise Strike (Ex), 3rd level: While she has at least 1 panache point, a swashbuckler gains the ability to strike precisely with a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon (though not natural weapon attacks), adding her swashbuckler level to damage. If she also has the precise strike deed from the duelist prestige class, the bonuses stack. This bonus damage is precision damage and is not multiplied on a critical hit. If the swashbuckler is beyond 5 feet from her target or uses a weapon other than a light or one-handed piercing weapon, she loses this bonus.

Swashbuckler Initiative (Ex), 3rd level: While she has at least 1 panache point, she gains a +2 bonus on initiative. If she also has Quick Draw, she can draw a light or one-handed piercing weapon as part of the initiative check.

Bleeding Wound (Ex), 7th level: When the swashbuckler hits a living creature with a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon, she can spend 1 panache point as a free action to have that attack deal bleed damage. Bleed damage equals the swashbuckler's Dexterity modifier (minimum 1). Alternatively, she can spend 2 panache points to cause 1 point of Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution bleed on a critical hit instead.

Evasive (Ex), 7th level: While the swashbuckler has at least 1 panache point, she gains the benefit of the evasion and uncanny dodge class features.

Subtle Blade (Ex), 7th level: While the swashbuckler has at least 1 panache point, she is immune to disarm, steal, and sunder combat maneuvers against any light or one-handed piercing melee weapons she wields.

Dizzying Defense (Ex), 11th level: While wielding a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon in one hand and nothing in the other, the swashbuckler can spend 1 panache point to take the fighting defensively option as a swift action rather than a standard action.

Perfect Thrust (Ex), 11th level: While the swashbuckler has at least 1 panache point, she can spend 2 panache to ignore all cover and concealment penalties on a single attack.

Swashbuckler's Edge (Ex), 11th level: While the swashbuckler has at least 1 panache point, she can take 10 on any Acrobatics, Climb, Escape Artist, Fly, Ride, or Swim check, even when in danger or distracted.

Cheat Death (Ex), 15th level: When the swashbuckler is reduced to 0 or fewer hp, she can spend all her remaining panache (minimum 1) to be instead reduced to 1 hp. She can use this ability once per day.

Deadly Stab (Ex), 15th level: When the swashbuckler confirms a critical hit against a creature using a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon, she can spend 2 panache points to force the target to make a Fortitude save (DC 10 + 1/2 the swashbuckler's level + her Dexterity modifier) or die.

Stunning Stab (Ex), 15th level: Once per day, when the swashbuckler hits a creature with a light or one-handed piercing melee weapon, she can spend 2 panache points to stun the creature for 1 round (Fort save negates, DC 10 + 1/2 level + Dex modifier).

Eternal Panache (Ex), 19th level: The swashbuckler's panache pool has a new maximum: Charisma modifier × 2 (minimum 2).

Swashbuckler Finesse (Ex)

At 1st level, a swashbuckler gains the benefits of the Weapon Finesse feat with light or one-handed piercing melee weapons, and she can use her Charisma score in place of Intelligence as a prerequisite for combat feats. This ability counts as having the Weapon Finesse feat for purposes of qualifying for other feats.

Charmed Life (Ex)

At 2nd level, the swashbuckler gains a knack for getting out of trouble. Three times per day as an immediate action before attempting a saving throw, she can add her Charisma modifier to the result of the save. She must choose to do this before the roll. At 6th level, she can do this four times per day, and at 10th level, five times per day. At 14th level, she can add her Charisma modifier to all saving throws without spending a daily use.

Nimble (Ex)

Starting at 3rd level, a swashbuckler gains a +1 dodge bonus to AC while wearing light or no armor. This bonus increases by +1 every four levels thereafter (maximum +5 at 19th level). Anything that causes the swashbuckler to lose her Dexterity bonus to AC also causes her to lose this dodge bonus.

Bonus Feats

At 4th level and every 4 levels thereafter, a swashbuckler gains a bonus feat in addition to those gained from normal advancement. These feats must be combat feats. The swashbuckler must meet all prerequisites.

Swashbuckler Weapon Training (Ex)

At 5th level, a swashbuckler gains a +1 bonus on attack and damage rolls with one-handed or light piercing melee weapons. This bonus increases by +1 every four levels after 5th. Counts as fighter weapon training for feat prerequisites.

Swashbuckler Weapon Mastery (Ex)

At 20th level, when wielding a light or one-handed piercing weapon, the swashbuckler gains the benefits of the Improved Critical feat and her attacks cannot be disarmed. All critical threats are automatically confirmed.
