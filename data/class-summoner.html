Summoner

APG Base Class

Role

Summoners form a powerful bond with a customizable outsider companion called an eidolon, which grows in power as the summoner levels. The summoner also gains access to a limited but potent arcane spell list with a strong conjuration focus. Together, summoner and eidolon form a devastating pair — the eidolon handles frontline combat while the summoner casts from range.

Alignment

Any.

Hit Died8
BABMedium (¾ level)
Good SavesWill
Poor SavesFortitude, Reflex
Skill Ranks2 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Craft (Int), Fly (Dex), Handle Animal (Cha), Knowledge (all) (Int), Linguistics (Int), Profession (Wis), Ride (Dex), Spellcraft (Int), Use Magic Device (Cha).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Summoners are proficient with all simple weapons. They are proficient with light armor. A summoner can cast summoner spells while wearing light armor without incurring spell failure. Like other arcane spellcasters, a summoner wearing medium or heavy armor incurs spell failure chance.

Spellcasting

A summoner casts arcane spells from the summoner spell list. He can cast any spell he knows without preparing ahead of time. To cast, he needs Charisma of at least 10 + the spell level. The DC is 10 + spell level + Charisma modifier. He knows a limited number of spells per level; the progression is similar to a sorcerer's but slower. He gains bonus spells per day for high Charisma.

Class Features

Cantrips

Summoners learn a number of cantrips as noted on the spells known table. These do not consume spell slots.

Eidolon (Ex)

A summoner begins play with the ability to summon to his side a powerful outsider called an eidolon. The eidolon forms a link with the summoner, who, forever after, summons an aspect of the same creature. An eidolon has the same alignment as the summoner that calls it. An eidolon is treated as a summoned creature, except that it is not sent back to its home plane until the eidolon is reduced to a number of negative hit points equal to or greater than its Constitution score. In addition, due to its tie to its summoner, an eidolon can touch and attack creatures warded by protection from evil and similar spells that prevent contact with summoned creatures.

A summoner can summon his eidolon in a ritual that takes 1 minute to perform. When summoned in this way, the eidolon hit points are unchanged from the last time it was dismissed or banished. The only exception to this is if the eidolon was slain, in which case it returns with half its normal hit points. The eidolon does not heal naturally. The eidolon remains until dismissed by the summoner (a standard action), or until it is sent back to its home plane due to death. If the eidolon is sent back to its home plane due to death, the summoner must wait 24 hours to resummon the eidolon. The eidolon cannot be sent back to its home plane by means of dispel magic, but spells such as dismissal and banishment work normally.

The eidolon has a number of evolutions that reflect its growth in power. An eidolon starts with a base form chosen from: biped, quadruped, or serpentine. It has a pool of evolution points to spend: 1 at 1st level, growing by 1 every level to a maximum of 20 at 20th level. Evolution points are spent on evolutions from a fixed list.

The eidolon's base statistics: HD = summoner level, BAB = summoner level, good saves = two (chosen at creation), base Str 16, Dex 12, Con 13, Int 7, Wis 10, Cha 11. It gains ability score increases at levels 4, 8, 12, 16, 20. Its natural armor bonus is equal to its summoner's level. It uses the summoner's base attack bonus. It gains feats as a normal creature. Its skill ranks equal 6 + Int modifier per HD.

Life Link (Su)

Starting at 1st level, a summoner forms a close bond with his eidolon. Whenever the eidolon takes enough damage to send it back to its home plane, the summoner can, as a free action, sacrifice any number of hit points. Each hit point sacrificed this way prevents 1 point of damage done to the eidolon. This can prevent the eidolon from being sent back to its home plane. The summoner and the eidolon must be within 100 feet of one another for this ability to be used.

Summon Monster I (Sp)

Starting at 1st level, a summoner can cast summon monster I as a spell-like ability a number of times per day equal to 3 + his Charisma modifier. He can cast this spell as a standard action and the creatures remain for 1 minute per level. At 3rd level and every 2 levels thereafter, the power of this ability increases by one spell level, allowing him to summon more powerful creatures (to a maximum of summon monster IX at 17th level). A summoner cannot have more than one summon monster or gate spell active in this way at one time. If this ability is used again, any existing summon immediately ends. These abilities are used as conjuration (summoning) spells.

Bond Senses (Su)

Starting at 2nd level, a summoner can, as a standard action, share the senses of his eidolon, hearing, seeing, smelling, tasting, and touching everything the eidolon does. He can use this ability a number of rounds per day equal to his summoner level. There is no range to this effect, but the eidolon and the summoner must be on the same plane. The summoner can end this effect as a free action.

Shield Ally (Ex)

At 4th level, whenever a summoner is within his eidolon's reach, the summoner receives a +2 shield bonus to his Armor Class and a +2 circumstance bonus on his saving throws. This bonus does not apply if the eidolon is grappled, helpless, paralyzed, stunned, or unconscious.

Maker's Call (Su)

At 6th level, as a standard action, a summoner can call his eidolon to his side. This functions as dimension door, using the summoner's caster level. When used, the eidolon appears adjacent to the summoner (or as close as possible if all adjacent spaces are occupied). If the eidolon is out of range, the ability is wasted. The summoner can use this ability once per day at 6th level, plus one additional time per day for every four levels thereafter.

Transposition (Su)

At 8th level, a summoner can use his maker's call ability to swap locations with his eidolon. If he does, the eidolon appears in the summoner's location and the summoner appears in the eidolon's last location, as if the two had exchanged positions.

Aspect (Su)

At 10th level, a summoner can divert up to 2 points from his eidolon's evolution pool to add evolutions to himself. He cannot select an evolution that the eidolon does not possess. The summoner must have the same base form as the eidolon to take evolutions that are form-dependent. The eidolon loses 1 evolution point from its pool for every 2 points diverted to the summoner.

Greater Shield Ally (Su)

At 12th level, whenever an ally is within the summoner's eidolon's reach, that ally receives a +2 shield bonus to their AC and a +2 circumstance bonus on saving throws. If the summoner is the ally, these bonuses increase to +4.

Life Bond (Su)

At 14th level, a summoner's life becomes linked to his eidolon's. As long as the eidolon is alive, the summoner is protected from death. If the summoner is reduced to negative hit points but not killed, he remains alive. Any damage over the amount needed to bring the summoner to –1 hit points is instead transferred to the eidolon. This transfer is instantaneous. The eidolon can be killed by this, sending it back to its home plane. Once the eidolon leaves, any healing that raises the summoner above –1 hit points stops this transfer.

Merge Forms (Su)

At 16th level, as a full-round action, a summoner can merge his body with that of his eidolon. When merged in this way, the summoner is protected from harm and can observe his surroundings via the eidolon. During this time, the eidolon is the only one that can be targeted by attacks. The summoner can remain merged for a number of rounds per day equal to his summoner level. He can end the merge as a free action. If the eidolon is dismissed during this time, the summoner is immediately ejected from the eidolon.

Greater Aspect (Su)

At 18th level, a summoner can divert more of his eidolon's evolutions to himself. He can now divert up to 6 evolution points (1-to-1 from the eidolon's pool).

Twin Eidolon (Su)

At 20th level, a summoner and his eidolon share a true connection. As a standard action, the summoner can assume the shape of his eidolon, gaining all of its evolutions, evolution points, and abilities for a number of minutes per day equal to his summoner level. His base form changes to that of the eidolon. The summoner can end this effect as a free action.
