Wizard

Core Class

Role

Wizards are the masters of arcane magic, drawing their power from intense study and a spellbook containing every spell they know. They are the most powerful spellcasters in the game at high levels, with access to the widest range of spells and the ability to prepare exactly the right magic for any situation. Their arcane school and bonded object or familiar further customize their role.

Alignment

Any.

Hit Died6
BABSlow (½ level)
Good SavesWill
Poor SavesFortitude, Reflex
Skill Ranks2 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Appraise (Int), Craft (Int), Fly (Dex), Knowledge (all) (Int), Linguistics (Int), Profession (Wis), Spellcraft (Int).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Wizards are proficient with the club, dagger, heavy crossbow, light crossbow, and quarterstaff, but not with any type of armor or shield. Armor of any type interferes with a wizard's movements, which can cause his spells with somatic components to fail.

Spellcasting

A wizard casts arcane spells drawn from the sorcerer/wizard spell list. A wizard must choose and prepare his spells ahead of time. To learn, prepare, or cast a spell, the wizard must have an Intelligence score equal to at least 10 + the spell level. The DC for saving throws is 10 + spell level + Intelligence modifier. Like other spellcasters, a wizard can cast only a certain number of spells of each spell level per day. His base daily spell allotment is on the table, plus bonus spells for high Intelligence. A wizard may know any number of spells, limited only by his spellbook. He prepares spells each day after 8 hours of rest, spending 1 hour studying his spellbook. A wizard begins play with a spellbook containing all 0-level wizard spells plus three 1st-level spells of his choice. For each point of Intelligence bonus the wizard has, the spellbook holds one additional 1st-level spell. At each new wizard level, he gains two new spells of any spell level or levels that he can cast, added to his spellbook for free.

Class Features

Arcane Bond (Ex or Sp)

At 1st level, wizards form a powerful bond with an object or a creature. This bond can take one of two forms: a familiar or a bonded object. A familiar is a magical pet that enhances the wizard's skills and senses and can deliver touch spells. A bonded object is an item a wizard can use once per day to cast any one spell in his spellbook, even if that spell is not prepared. If a bonded object is lost or destroyed, it can be replaced after 1 week in a special ritual costing 200 gp per wizard level plus the cost of the masterwork item. A wizard can use his bonded object to cast any spell from his spellbook, even if he hasn't prepared it (but must have a slot available of the appropriate level).

Arcane School

A wizard can choose to specialize in one school of magic, gaining additional spells and powers based on that school. Specialization requires giving up access to two opposition schools. Alternatively, a wizard can be a universalist, mastering no school but gaining the ability to prepare one extra spell of any level per day. Schools: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, Transmutation. Each school grants two bonus spells per day (one per spell level slot) and a set of school powers gained at 1st, 6th, 11th, and 20th level.

Cantrips

Wizards can prepare a number of cantrips each day. These spells are cast like any other spell, but they are not expended when cast and may be used again. A wizard can prepare a cantrip from his spellbook any number of times, but each preparation fills a cantrip spell slot.

Scribe Scroll

At 1st level, a wizard gains Scribe Scroll as a bonus feat.

Bonus Feats

At 5th level and every five levels thereafter (10th, 15th, and 20th), a wizard gains a bonus feat. These feats must be selected from the following list: Craft Magic Arms and Armor, Craft Ring, Craft Rod, Craft Staff, Craft Wand, Craft Wondrous Item, Empower Spell, Enlarge Spell, Extend Spell, Forge Ring, Heighten Spell, Improved Counterspell, Magical Aptitude, Maximize Spell, Quicken Spell, Scribe Scroll, Silent Spell, Spell Focus, Spell Mastery, Spell Penetration, Still Spell, Widen Spell. He does not need to meet the prerequisites for these feats.

Spellbooks

A wizard must study his spellbook each day to prepare his spells. He cannot prepare any spell not recorded in his spellbook, except for read magic, which all wizards can prepare from memory. A wizard begins play with a spellbook containing all 0-level wizard spells plus three 1st-level spells. Adding a new spell to a spellbook costs 100 gp per spell level (for materials) and takes 1 hour per spell level. Copying a spell from a scroll destroys the scroll. A wizard can also learn spells by copying them from another wizard's spellbook (with the originating wizard's permission), costing 50 gp per spell level and 1 hour per spell level.
