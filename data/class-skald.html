Skald

ACG Hybrid Class (Bard/Barbarian)

Role

Skalds combine the barbarian's rage with the bard's bardic performance. Their raging song functions like bardic performance, but instead of morale bonuses they grant a version of the rage ability to allies. This makes the skald an ideal martial support character — fighting in the front line while making everyone around them stronger. They also cast arcane spells.

Alignment

Any non-lawful.

Hit Died8
BABMedium (¾ level)
Good SavesFortitude, Will
Poor SavesReflex
Skill Ranks4 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Acrobatics (Dex), Bluff (Cha), Climb (Str), Craft (Int), Diplomacy (Cha), Escape Artist (Dex), Handle Animal (Cha), Intimidate (Cha), Knowledge (all) (Int), Linguistics (Int), Perception (Wis), Perform (Cha), Profession (Wis), Ride (Dex), Sense Motive (Wis), Spellcraft (Int), Swim (Str), Use Magic Device (Cha).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Skalds are proficient with all simple and martial weapons, light armor, medium armor, and shields (except tower shields). A skald can cast skald spells while wearing light or medium armor and using a shield without arcane spell failure.

Spellcasting

A skald casts arcane spells drawn from the bard spell list. He can cast any spell he knows without preparing it ahead of time. To cast a spell, a skald must have a Charisma score of at least 10 + the spell level. DC is 10 + spell level + Charisma modifier. A skald knows a limited number of spells: 4 cantrips and 2 first-level spells at 1st level, gaining additional spells known at each level up.

Class Features

Raging Song (Su)

A skald is trained to use music, words, and rhythmic chanting to inspire his allies to feats of strength and ferocity. Beginning at 1st level, a skald can use raging song a number of rounds per day equal to 3 + his Charisma modifier. At each level after 1st, he can use raging song for 2 additional rounds per day. Starting a raging song is a move action, but it can be maintained each round as a free action. A raging song cannot be disrupted, but it ends immediately if the skald is killed, paralyzed, stunned, knocked unconscious, or otherwise unable to maintain it. A skald cannot have more than one raging song in effect at one time. At 7th level, starting a raging song is a swift action. At 13th level, it's a free action.

Inspired Rage: At 1st level, affected allies gain a +2 morale bonus to Strength and Constitution and a +1 morale bonus on Will saves, but also a –1 penalty to AC (as the barbarian's rage). Unlike barbarian rage, there is no limit on skill use, but the ally still cannot cast spells while benefiting from inspired rage unless the skald is 8th level or higher (when he can allow allies to cast while in inspired rage). Allies who choose to benefit from inspired rage must spend a free action to activate it. Allies may end inspired rage as a free action on their turn. The moral bonuses increase at 4th level (+4 Str, +4 Con, +2 Will) and at 8th level (+6 Str, +6 Con, +3 Will). At 20th level the bonuses are +8 Str, +8 Con, +4 Will.

Song of Marching: At 6th level, a skald can use raging song to grant endurance to allies within 60 feet. Affected allies can hustle for 1 hour without taking nonlethal damage or suffering a penalty to their Constitution score.

Song of Strength: At 6th level, a skald can use raging song to grant allies within 30 feet a +1 bonus on Strength checks, Strength-based skill checks, and combat maneuver checks. The bonus increases to +2 at 12th level.

Dirge of Doom: At 8th level, as the bard ability.

Song of the Fallen: At 10th level, a skald can use raging song to conjure spectral warriors. Once per round as a free action, he can summon one undead warrior (as the spell create undead, but the creature has Hit Dice equal to the skald's level) as long as the raging song continues. Each warrior disappears when the song ends.

Spell Warrior: At 14th level, a skald can expend a round of raging song as a free action to grant nearby allies the ability to cast one spell they know as if they were not raging.

Bardic Knowledge (Ex)

A skald adds half his class level (minimum 1) to all Knowledge skill checks and may make all Knowledge skill checks untrained, as the bard ability.

Well-Versed (Ex)

At 2nd level, the skald gains a +4 bonus on saving throws against bardic performance, sonic, and language-dependent effects, as the bard ability.

Rage Powers (Ex)

At 2nd level, and every 4 levels thereafter, a skald learns a rage power. He can use this power himself while using inspired rage, and can grant it to allies affected by his inspired rage. He cannot select a rage power that has a prerequisite he does not meet. At 2nd level, he can select any standard barbarian rage power except for come and get me, fearless rage, mighty swing, roused anger, and unexpected strike. At 10th level, he can also select greater rage powers (those that require the barbarian to be 12th level).

Uncanny Dodge (Ex)

At 2nd level, the skald cannot be caught flat-footed and does not lose Dex to AC against invisible attackers.

Improved Uncanny Dodge (Ex)

At 5th level, the skald can no longer be flanked.

Lore Master (Ex)

At 5th level, the skald can take 10 on any Knowledge skill check, as the bard ability.

Damage Reduction (Ex)

At 7th level, the skald gains DR 1/—, increasing by 1 at 10th, 13th, 16th, and 19th level.

Jack of All Trades (Ex)

At 10th level, the skald can use any skill, even if the skill normally requires training.

Inspired Rage (Greater)

At 12th level, the skald's inspired rage ability improves further. The Strength and Constitution morale bonuses become +6 (+8 at 16th, +10 at 20th).
