Investigator

ACG Hybrid Class (Alchemist/Rogue)

Role

Investigators combine the alchemist's studied preparation with the rogue's skill mastery. They use studied strike to deliver massive burst damage when they've taken a moment to assess a foe, and their inspirations fuel a wide range of knowledge and skill abilities. Investigators are premier knowledge characters and skilled combatants who reward careful play.

Alignment

Any.

Hit Died8
BABMedium (¾ level)
Good SavesReflex, Will
Poor SavesFortitude
Skill Ranks6 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Acrobatics (Dex), Appraise (Int), Bluff (Cha), Climb (Str), Craft (Int), Diplomacy (Cha), Disable Device (Dex), Disguise (Cha), Escape Artist (Dex), Heal (Wis), Intimidate (Cha), Knowledge (all) (Int), Linguistics (Int), Perception (Wis), Perform (Cha), Profession (Wis), Sense Motive (Wis), Sleight of Hand (Dex), Spellcraft (Int), Stealth (Dex), Swim (Str), Use Magic Device (Cha).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Investigators are proficient with all simple weapons, plus the hand crossbow, rapier, sap, short sword, and shortbow. They are proficient with light armor but not shields.

Extracts

An investigator prepares and uses extracts exactly as an alchemist does, using his investigator level as his alchemist level for determining his formulae list and preparing extracts. He begins with a formula book containing six 1st-level extracts and adds two new extracts per level (any level available to him). He can prepare a number of extracts per day per the table, based on Intelligence.

Class Features

Inspiration (Ex)

An investigator is beyond talented — he also possesses a knack for making deductions. Inspiration is an extraordinary ability that allows an investigator to augment skill checks and ability checks. The investigator has an inspiration pool equal to 1/2 his investigator level + his Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). As a free action, he can expend one use of inspiration from his pool to add 1d6 to the result of the check, after the check is rolled but before the results are revealed. He can use inspiration on any Knowledge, Linguistics, or Spellcraft skill check without expending a use of inspiration. Inspiration can also be used on attack rolls and saving throws (costing 2 uses each).

Trapfinding (Ex)

An investigator adds 1/2 his level to Perception checks made to locate traps and to Disable Device checks (minimum +1). He can use Disable Device to disarm magic traps.

Alchemy (Su)

At 1st level, an investigator gains the alchemist's alchemy class feature, including the Craft (alchemy) bonus and the ability to identify potions.

Studied Combat (Ex)

With a keen eye and quick mind, an investigator can assess the situation and strike where it counts. As a move action, an investigator can study a single enemy he can see. Upon doing so, he adds 1/2 his investigator level as an insight bonus on melee attack rolls and as a bonus on damage rolls against the creature. This effect lasts for a number of rounds equal to his Intelligence modifier (minimum 1) or until he deals studied strike damage, whichever comes first. The bonus on damage rolls is precision damage and is not multiplied on a critical hit. An investigator can only have one target studied at a time, and once a creature has been studied, he cannot study that creature again for 24 hours.

Studied Strike (Ex)

At 4th level, an investigator can choose to make a studied strike against the target of his studied combat as a free action, upon successfully hitting his studied target with a melee attack, to deal additional damage. The damage is 1d6 at 4th level, plus 1d6 for every 2 levels thereafter (2d6 at 6th, 3d6 at 8th, 4d6 at 10th, 5d6 at 12th, 6d6 at 14th, 7d6 at 16th, 8d6 at 18th, 9d6 at 20th). This is precision damage and is not multiplied on a critical hit. Studied strike can only be used once per studied combat.

Investigator Talents

At 3rd level and every 2 levels thereafter, an investigator gains one investigator talent. Some talents have prerequisites and cannot be selected until those are met. A talent may only be selected once unless noted otherwise. Notable talents include: Alchemist Discovery, Amazing Inspiration, Combat Inspiration, Eidetic Recollection, Empathy, Expanded Inspiration, Extended Inspiration, Familiar, Feat, Greater Combat Inspiration, Infusion, Item Lore, Perceptive Tracking, Poison Lore, Quick Study, Rogue Talent, Sapping Offensive, Sleight of Hand, Stealing Initiative, Tenacious Inspiration, Theorist, Toppling Strike, Trap Sense, Underworld Inspiration.

Poison Lore (Ex)

At 2nd level, an investigator has a deep understanding and/or appreciation for poisons. He cannot accidentally poison himself when applying poison to a weapon. He also gains a +2 insight bonus on Craft (alchemy) checks made to craft poisons and a +4 insight bonus on saving throws against poisons.

Keen Recollection

At 3rd level, an investigator can attempt all Knowledge skill checks untrained.

Trap Sense (Ex)

At 3rd level, an investigator gains a +1 bonus on Reflex saves and AC against traps, increasing by +1 every 3 levels.

Swift Alchemy (Ex)

At 3rd level, an investigator can craft alchemical items at half the normal time and apply poison as a move action.

Ceaseless Observation (Ex)

At 4th level, an investigator's ability to notice fine details is remarkable. He uses his Intelligence modifier instead of Wisdom on Perception checks, and adds his Intelligence modifier in addition to Wisdom on Sense Motive checks.

Uncanny Dodge (Ex)

At 4th level, an investigator cannot be caught flat-footed and does not lose Dex to AC if attacked by an invisible opponent.

Improved Uncanny Dodge (Ex)

At 8th level, the investigator can no longer be flanked.

True Inspiration (Ex)

At 20th level, an investigator can use inspiration on all skill checks without expending uses of inspiration. In addition, whenever he expends inspiration on an ability check, attack roll, saving throw, or skill check, he adds 2d6 rather than 1d6 to the result.
