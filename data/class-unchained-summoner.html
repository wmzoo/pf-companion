Summoner (Unchained)

Pathfinder Unchained

Role

The unchained summoner fixes the notorious power imbalance of the original summoner by tying the eidolon to a specific outsider subtype (angel, demon, devil, etc.) which determines available evolutions. This prevents the most overpowered combinations. The spell list is also restricted to a summoner-specific list rather than the full sorcerer/wizard list. The unchained version is required in most organized play.

Any.

Class Skills and Proficiencies

Same as the original summoner. Light armor only, no arcane spell failure in light armor.

Key Differences from Original Summoner

The unchained summoner must choose a subtype for his eidolon at 1st level from the following: agathion, angel, azata, daemon, demon, devil, div, elemental, inevitable, protean, psychopomp, qlippoth, rakshasa, or shadow. This subtype determines which evolutions the eidolon can take and sets the eidolon's alignment. The summoner's alignment must be compatible with his eidolon's subtype (e.g., a demon eidolon requires an evil or neutral summoner). The unchained summoner also uses his own spell list rather than the sorcerer/wizard list.

Class Features

Eidolon (Ex)

As the original summoner, except the eidolon has a set base form and list of available evolutions determined by its subtype. Each subtype has its own base form (biped, quadruped, or serpentine), and a specific list of evolutions it can take. Some evolutions are available to all subtypes, while others are exclusive to specific ones. The eidolon cannot take evolutions outside its subtype's allowed list.

Evolution Pool

The eidolon gains evolution points based on summoner level as follows: 1 (1st), 2 (2nd), 3 (3rd), 4 (4th), 5 (5th), 6 (6th), 7 (7th), 8 (8th), 9 (9th), 11 (10th), 12 (11th), 13 (12th), 14 (13th), 15 (14th), 16 (15th), 17 (16th), 18 (17th), 19 (18th), 20 (19th), 23 (20th).
