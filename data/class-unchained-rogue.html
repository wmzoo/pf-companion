Rogue (Unchained)

Pathfinder Unchained

Role

The unchained rogue is a significant upgrade to the core rogue. It gains Dexterity-to-damage via the finesse training ability, debilitating injury to penalize targets hit by sneak attacks, and rogue's edge to master specific skills. These changes close the gap between the rogue and other martial classes substantially. This is the preferred version for new play.

Alignment

Any.

Hit Died8
BABMedium (¾ level)
Good SavesReflex
Poor SavesFortitude, Will
Skill Ranks8 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills and Proficiencies

Identical to core rogue. Same weapons and armor.

Class Features

Sneak Attack

As core rogue. 1d6 at 1st level, +1d6 every 2 levels.

Trapfinding

As core rogue.

Finesse Training (Ex)

At 1st level, an unchained rogue gains Weapon Finesse as a bonus feat. In addition, starting at 3rd level, she can select any one type of weapon that can be used with Weapon Finesse (such as rapier, shortsword, or unarmed strike). Once this choice is made, it cannot be changed. Whenever she makes a successful melee attack with the selected weapon, she adds her Dexterity modifier instead of her Strength modifier to the damage roll. If any effect would prevent the rogue from adding her Strength modifier to the damage roll, she does not add her Dexterity modifier. The rogue can select a second weapon at 11th level and a third at 19th level.

Evasion (Ex)

At 2nd level, as core rogue.

Rogue Talents

At 2nd level and every 2 levels thereafter, as core rogue. The full list of core rogue talents is available, plus several unchained-specific talents.

Danger Sense (Ex)

At 3rd level, an unchained rogue gains a +1 bonus on Reflex saves to avoid traps and a +1 dodge bonus to AC against attacks by traps. In addition, she gains a +1 bonus on Perception checks to avoid being surprised by a foe. These bonuses increase by +1 every 3 rogue levels. This replaces trap sense.

Debilitating Injury (Ex)

At 4th level, whenever an unchained rogue deals sneak attack damage to a foe, she can also debilitate the target of her attack, causing one of the following penalties to apply to the target until the start of the rogue's next turn. The rogue can choose which condition to apply after the attack roll has been confirmed as a hit. The effects of debilitating injury don't stack with themselves, but additional attacks that deal sneak attack damage extend the duration by 1 round.

Bewildered: The target becomes bewildered, taking a –2 penalty to AC. The target takes an additional –2 penalty to AC against all attacks made by the rogue. At 10th level and 16th level, the penalty to AC against attacks made by the rogue increases by –2 (total –6 at 16th).

Disoriented: The target takes a –2 penalty on attack rolls. In addition, the target takes an additional –2 penalty on all attack rolls it makes against the rogue. At 10th level and 16th level, the penalty to attack rolls against the rogue increases by –2 (total –6 at 16th).

Hampered: All of the target's speeds are reduced by half (minimum 5 feet). In addition, the target cannot take a 5-foot step.

Uncanny Dodge (Ex)

At 4th level, as core rogue.

Rogue's Edge (Ex)

At 5th level, an unchained rogue has mastered a single skill beyond that skill's normal boundaries, gaining results that others can't hope to match. She gains the skill unlock powers for that skill as appropriate for her number of ranks in that skill. At 10th, 15th, and 20th levels, she chooses an additional skill and gains skill unlock powers for that skill as well. The skills available include every skill in the game — the skill unlock powers are detailed in the Pathfinder Unchained rulebook and include abilities that trigger at 5, 10, 15, and 20 ranks in a skill.

Improved Uncanny Dodge (Ex)

At 8th level, as core rogue.

Advanced Talents (Ex)

At 10th level and every 2 levels thereafter, as core rogue.

Master Strike (Ex)

At 20th level, as core rogue (sleep, paralysis, or death on sneak attack).
