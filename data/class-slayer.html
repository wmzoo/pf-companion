Slayer

ACG Hybrid Class (Ranger/Rogue)

Role

Slayers are professional hunters and killers who combine the ranger's studied target ability with the rogue's precision damage. They are skilled martial combatants who excel at taking down specific marks with methodical precision. With an extensive combat feat list and sneak attack, they're damage dealers who require no magical preparation.

Alignment

Any.

Hit Died10
BABFull (1 per level)
Good SavesFortitude, Reflex
Poor SavesWill
Skill Ranks6 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Acrobatics (Dex), Bluff (Cha), Climb (Str), Craft (Int), Disguise (Cha), Heal (Wis), Intimidate (Cha), Knowledge (dungeoneering) (Int), Knowledge (geography) (Int), Knowledge (local) (Int), Knowledge (nature) (Int), Perception (Wis), Profession (Wis), Ride (Dex), Sense Motive (Wis), Stealth (Dex), Survival (Wis), Swim (Str).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Slayers are proficient with all simple and martial weapons, light armor, medium armor, and shields (except tower shields).

Class Features

Studied Target (Ex)

A slayer can study an opponent he can see as a move action. The slayer then gains a +1 bonus on Bluff, Knowledge, Perception, Sense Motive, and Survival checks attempted against that opponent, and a +1 bonus on weapon attack and damage rolls against it. The DCs of slayer class abilities against that opponent increase by 1. A slayer can only maintain these bonuses against one opponent at a time; these bonuses remain in effect until either the opponent is dead or the slayer studies a new target. If a slayer deals sneak attack damage to a target, he can study that target as an immediate action, allowing him to apply his studied target bonuses against that target (including to the normal weapon damage roll). At 5th, 10th, 15th, and 20th levels, the bonuses on weapon attack and damage rolls, Bluff, Knowledge, Perception, Sense Motive, and Survival checks increase by 1. In addition, at each such interval, the slayer is able to maintain these bonuses against an additional studied target at the same time. The slayer may discard this connection to a studied target as a free action, allowing him to study another target in its place. At 7th level, a slayer can study an opponent as a move or swift action.

Track (Ex)

A slayer adds 1/2 his level (minimum 1) to Survival skill checks made to follow or identify tracks.

Sneak Attack (Ex)

At 3rd level, if a slayer catches an opponent unable to defend himself effectively from his attack, he can strike a vital spot for extra damage. This functions as the rogue's sneak attack ability. The extra damage is 1d6 at 3rd level and increases by 1d6 every 3 levels thereafter.

Slayer Talents

At 2nd level and every 2 levels thereafter, a slayer gains a slayer talent. Unless otherwise noted, a talent may only be chosen once. Most slayer talents are rogue talents. The slayer uses his slayer level as his rogue level for the purpose of meeting the talent's prerequisites. Slayer-specific talents include: Blood Reader, Deadly Range, Foil Scrutiny, Hard to Fool, Ranger Combat Style, Slowing Strike, Terrain Mastery, Trapfinding, and others. At 10th level, he can also take advanced rogue talents or slayer advanced talents.

Ranger Combat Style

At 2nd level, a slayer can select a ranger combat style to gain a bonus combat feat. He does not need to meet the prerequisites for these feats while using this style. He gains additional style feats at 6th, 10th, 14th, and 18th level.

Favored Target (Ex)

At 5th level, the slayer designates one creature type as a favored target. He gains a +2 bonus on Bluff, Knowledge, Perception, Sense Motive, and Survival checks against creatures of this type, and a +2 bonus on weapon attack and damage rolls against them. He can also make Knowledge checks against favored target creatures untrained. At 10th, 15th, and 20th level, he can designate an additional favored target and increase any one existing favored target bonus by +2.

Quarry (Ex)

At 14th level, the slayer gains the quarry ability, as the ranger.

Swift Studied Target (Ex)

At 7th level, studying a target becomes a swift action. At 14th level, the slayer can study a target as a free action.

Improved Quarry (Ex)

At 19th level, the slayer's quarry ability improves, as the ranger ability.

Master Slayer (Ex)

At 20th level, the slayer becomes a master at capturing or killing his studied targets. As a standard action, he can make a single attack against a studied target, dealing damage as if he'd made a full attack. If the attack hits, the target must make a Fortitude save (DC 10 + 1/2 the slayer's level + the slayer's Intelligence modifier) or die. Whether or not the save succeeds, the target is immune to this ability for 24 hours.
