Warpriest

ACG Hybrid Class (Cleric/Fighter)

Role

Warpriests are militant divine servants who blend a fighter's martial ability with condensed divine magic. Their blessings — drawn from their deity's domains — provide powerful swift-action buffs that keep the warpriest competitive in melee while also contributing divine utility. They are excellent frontline divine combatants with meaningful self-buffing capability.

Alignment

Within one step of deity.

Hit Died8
BABMedium (¾ level)
Good SavesFortitude, Will
Poor SavesReflex
Skill Ranks2 + Int modifier per level

Class Skills

Climb (Str), Craft (Int), Diplomacy (Cha), Heal (Wis), Intimidate (Cha), Knowledge (engineering) (Int), Knowledge (religion) (Int), Profession (Wis), Ride (Dex), Sense Motive (Wis), Spellcraft (Int), Survival (Wis), Swim (Str).

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

A warpriest is proficient with all simple and martial weapons, with all armor, and with shields (except tower shields). He also gains proficiency with the favored weapon of his deity. A warpriest can cast warpriest spells while wearing any armor without incurring arcane spell failure.

Spellcasting

A warpriest casts divine spells drawn from the cleric spell list. His alignment may restrict certain spells. A warpriest must choose and prepare spells ahead of time, gaining them through prayer. He must have a Wisdom score of at least 10 + the spell level. DC is 10 + spell level + Wisdom modifier. He receives bonus spells for high Wisdom. He prepares spells after 8 hours of rest. He uses a condensed spell progression: slower than a cleric but with full access to the list.

Class Features

Aura (Ex)

A warpriest has a particularly powerful aura corresponding to his deity's alignment (as the cleric ability).

Blessings (Su)

A warpriest's deity grants him two blessings based on two domains chosen from among those belonging to his deity. Each blessing grants a minor power and a major power. Using a blessing is a swift action unless stated otherwise. The warpriest can call upon the power of his blessings a number of times per day equal to 3 + his Wisdom modifier. Each time he calls upon a blessing, it counts against this total. The powers granted by a blessing are based on the warpriest's level. Minor blessings function at reduced effect and major blessings are available only from 10th level onward.

Example — War Blessing: Minor power: weapon of the deity (imbue weapon with +1 enhancement and sacred damage for 1 minute). Major power (10th+): battle rage (grant ally within 30 ft. a +4 morale bonus on attack/damage for 1 round per 5 levels).

Example — Good Blessing: Minor power: holy strike (weapon deals +1d6 vs. evil for 1 minute). Major power: holy weapons (all allies' weapons deal +1d6 vs. evil for 1 minute).

Full blessing list includes: Air, Animal, Artifice, Chaos, Charm, Community, Darkness, Death, Destruction, Earth, Evil, Fire, Glory, Good, Healing, Knowledge, Law, Liberation, Luck, Madness, Magic, Nobility, Plant, Protection, Repose, Rune, Scalykind, Strength, Sun, Travel, Trickery, War, Water, Weather, plus many others.

Focus Weapon

At 1st level, a warpriest receives Weapon Focus as a bonus feat (does not need to meet prerequisites). He must choose the deity's favored weapon or his own deity's weapon for this feat.

Sacred Weapon (Su)

At 1st level, weapons wielded by a warpriest are charged with the power of his faith. In addition to the favored weapon of his deity, the warpriest can designate any weapon as a sacred weapon by selecting that weapon with the Weapon Focus feat; if he has multiple Weapon Focus feats, this ability applies to all of them. Whenever the warpriest hits with his sacred weapon, the weapon damage is based on his level and not the weapon type. The warpriest can decide to use the weapon's base damage instead of the sacred weapon damage—this must be declared before the attack roll is made. The damage for a Medium warpriest: 1d6 (1st–4th), 1d8 (5th–9th), 1d10 (10th–14th), 2d6 (15th–19th), 2d8 (20th). As a swift action, a warpriest can add one of the following properties to his sacred weapon (this lasts for 1 minute): dancing, flaming, flaming burst, frost, icy burst, keen, shock, shocking burst, speed, or vorpal. He can have one property active at a time. Using this ability costs one use of his blessings per minute. The property added must be one that can be added to that weapon type. The warpriest must have the sacred weapon enhancement at least equal to the bonus cost of the added property.

Sacred Armor (Su)

At 7th level, the warpriest gains the ability to enhance his armor with divine power as a swift action. This enhancement lasts for 1 minute per warpriest level. When used, the warpriest can add one of the following armor properties: energy resistance (one type), ghost touch, heavy fortification, invulnerability, light fortification, medium fortification, or spell resistance. He must expend one use of his blessings. The enhancement bonus increases at higher levels: +1 (7th), +2 (9th), +3 (11th), +4 (13th), +5 (15th).

Channel Energy (Su)

Starting at 4th level, a warpriest can release a wave of energy. This functions as the cleric's channel energy ability but uses a d6 instead of d6+level. He channels positive energy if good-aligned, negative if evil. He can do this a number of times per day equal to 1/2 his warpriest level + his Charisma modifier.

Bonus Feats

At 3rd level and every 3 levels thereafter, a warpriest gains a bonus feat in addition to those gained from normal advancement. These must be combat feats. He must meet all prerequisites.

Aspect of War (Su)

At 20th level, the warpriest can channel an aspect of war, becoming an unstoppable force for 1 round per warpriest level per day. While channeling, he gains a +6 sacred (or profane) bonus to Strength, Constitution, and Wisdom; his weapon attacks deal maximum damage, all critical threats are automatically confirmed, and he gains regeneration 10 (overcome by the energy type opposed to his deity).
